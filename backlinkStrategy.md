## 8. Broken Link Building

### Process
1. Find broken links on eCommerce/Shopify blogs
2. Create/identify your content that could replace it
3. Reach out to site owner

### Tools
- Ahrefs Broken Link Checker (free)
- Check My Links (Chrome extension)
- Dead Link Checker

### Target Queries
- "Shopify apps" + "404"
- "product recommendation tools" on high-authority sites

### Outreach Template
```
Subject: Broken Link on [Page Title]

Hi [Name],

I was reading your article "[Title]" and noticed a broken link to [topic].

I actually wrote a comprehensive guide on the same topic that might be a 
good replacement: [your URL]

Let me know if you'd like to update it. Happy to help!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Scan 20 top eCommerce blogs for broken links
- [ ] Create replacement content if needed
- [ ] Send outreach emails

## Notes

- **Quality > Quantity**: One backlink from a DR70 site beats 10 from DR20 sites
- **Relevance Matters**: eCommerce/Shopify backlinks are worth more than general directories
- **Be Patient**: Backlink building takes 3-6 months to show SEO impact
- **Stay Consistent**: Better to get 5 quality backlinks per month consistently than 50 in one month then nothing
